What Is Bliss?

True existence, pure consciousness, infinite joy — the nature of being

सच्चिदानन्द
Sat-Chit-Ananda — Existence-Consciousness-Bliss

Bliss is not pleasure, not happiness, not ecstasy. Ananda is the nature of reality itself — the infinite joy that arises when you recognize your true self. It requires nothing, lacks nothing, fears nothing. It IS the ground of all being.

How 8 Traditions Describe Divine Bliss

VEDIC TRADITION
आनन्द (Ananda - Divine Bliss)

The third aspect of Brahman — Sat-Chit-Ananda (Being-Consciousness-Bliss). Ananda is not dependent on circumstances; it is the inherent nature of the Self, revealed when the illusion of separation dissolves.

BUDDHIST TRADITION
निर्वाण (Nirvana - Extinction of Suffering)

The cessation of craving, aversion, and ignorance. Nirvana is not annihilation but liberation — "the unborn, unmade, unconditioned." When suffering ends, what remains is peace beyond understanding.

CHRISTIAN TRADITION
Beatific Vision / Theosis

"Rejoice always, pray without ceasing, give thanks in all circumstances" (1 Thessalonians 5:16-18). Ultimate joy comes from union with God — the Beatific Vision where the soul beholds divine glory eternally.

SUFI TRADITION
Sukr / Wajd (Divine Ecstasy)

The ecstatic joy of union with the Beloved (Allah). As Rumi sang: "When you do things from your soul, you feel a river moving in you, a joy." Bliss is the dissolution of the self in divine love.

TAOIST TRADITION
自在 (Zìzài - Natural Freedom)

The effortless joy of Wu Wei (non-doing). When you align with the Tao, life flows like water — spontaneous, harmonious, free. Bliss is not sought; it arises naturally.

SIKH TRADITION
आनंद (Anand - Divine Joy)

"In divine bliss, the mind merges with God" (Guru Granth Sahib). True happiness comes from remembering the Divine Name (Naam Simran) and serving others (Seva).

JEWISH TRADITION
שִׂמְחָה (Simcha - Sacred Joy)

"Serve the Lord with gladness" (Psalm 100:2). Joy is a divine commandment — not frivolous happiness but deep spiritual celebration of existence itself. The soul's delight in returning to its source.

JAIN TRADITION
मोक्ष (Moksha - Liberation)

Complete freedom from karma and the cycle of rebirth. The liberated soul (Siddha) experiences infinite knowledge, infinite perception, infinite bliss, and infinite power — its true nature restored.
